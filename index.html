The giant Vatanayokudl glacier is in Iceland and has the status of the largest in Europe, taking third place in the world after Greenland and Antarctica glaciers. In his South Parts are amazing ice caves of extraordinary beauty. They have been formed for many thousands of years under the influence of underground geothermal sources and flows of melt water. As a result of melting and freezing, the cave walls accepted amazing forms and shimmer in thousands of shades-from bright blue, aquamarine and Indigo to orange-red, when the rays of the sun penetrate the thickness of the ice. Lake Baikal

Ancient Siberian Baikal suppresses its grandeur, beauty and unfortunate expanses. It was recognized as the deepest lake in the world, and for its unique ecosystem was included in the UNESCO World Heritage List. Stunning natural landscapes of Baikal can be admired at any time of the year - the climate in this region of Eastern Siberia is soft enough for fascinating trips and walks. One of the most popular tourist places here is the village of Listvyanka. A large number of excursions to the most interesting places are sent from here. In the list of the most popular-Barguzinsky and Chivyrkuyui Gulfs, a rock chaman-stone, the Holy Nose Peninsula, Olkhon Island and the Small Sea. For fans of outdoor activities, horseback riding, skiing, lake cruises and campaigns along the Big Baikal Trail (BBT) - a system of ecological paths that allow you to admire the unique beauty of this region.
